Kyrgyzstan

The Marvelous City

Bishkek

Known for its wide, tree-lined boulevards and a mix of Soviet-style architecture and modern developments. It is often described as one of the greenest cities in Central Asia due to its many parks like Oak Park and Panfilov Park.

Bishkek’s urban layout is defined by a historic rectangular grid of tree-lined boulevards and traditional irrigation channels. The city’s architecture features a unique blend of Soviet-era functionalism, Stalinist monuments, and modern developments like the Ottoman-style Imam Sarakhsi Central Mosque.

Iconic Kyrgyzstan: The Must-See Spots

Yurt Camp

Nestled in the lush emerald-green alpine meadows known as Jailoos, these traditional Yurts (Boz-ui) offer a sophisticated, eco-friendly sanctuary that blends ancient nomadic heritage with the serene majesty of the Kyrgyz mountains. .

Kaji-Say Canyons

Tucked away on the southern shore of Lake Issyk-Kul, Mars Canyon is a breathtaking labyrinth of fiery red sandstone and dramatic ridges that so closely mimics the Martian landscape it feels like stepping onto another planet.

Kel-Suu Lake

Gorgeous Lake Kel-Suu was formed thousands years ago due to destruction of the mountain massif. It is located in Kok-Kiya valley (3 520 m), Naryn region, close to the border with China.

Osh Bazaar

As the vibrant soul of Bishkek, Osh Bazaar is a sprawling sensory masterpiece where the aromas of tandoor-fresh bread and exotic spices mingle with the kaleidoscopic colors of traditional Kyrgyz felt-work and mountain-grown fruits.
